Word: Definition:
pathos An emotional appeal. Attemps to evoke an emotional response in the audience.
logos A logical appeal. Attemps to use logic and reason to persuade the audience.
ethos An appeal to credibility, or character. Attemps to convince the audience that the creator of the message can be trusted.
bandwagon Claims that because many people do something, or because something is popular it is valid.
Slippery slope Argument in which a relatively small (or insignificant) step leads to something of bigger consequence (usually negative).
appeal to traiditon Occurs when it is assumed that something is better or correct simply because it is older, traditional, or "always has been done."
straw man Misrepresenting, or ignoring someone's actual argument to make it easier to attack.
appeal to authority Implies or states that because an authority believes something, it must be true. Sometimes, the 'authority' has no expertise in the area in question.
Word: Definition:

conjunction connects words, clauses, or sentences

adjective describes a noun
adverb describes a verb; often ends in -ly
verb expresses action or being
noun person, place, thing, or idea

































Word: Definition:
Literary Non-fiction genre of writing that uses literary syles and techniques to create factually accurate narratives. Also known as creative non-fiction or narrative nonfiction.
narrative a story, real or imaginary, often written or told in a sequence (order).
diary writing with discrete (separate) entries arranged by date. Often reports real life events.
personal journal much more personal than a diary. Not just a record of experiences, but includes feelings, emotions, problems, reflections.
memoir a collection of memories that an individual writes about moments or events, both public or private. Allegedly autobiographical.
essay a short piece of writing on a particular subject, usually in prose.
author's purpose what the author wanted to do, his/her intent either to inform or teach someone about something, to entertain people, or to persuade or convince the audience to do or not to do something.
climax the turning point in a narrative, the moment when the conflict is as its most intense. Typically, the structure of stories, novels, and plays is one of rising action in which tension builds to the climax.
conflict/problem struggle or clash between opposing characters, forces, or emotions.
tone the author or speaker's emotional attitude towards the topic.
audience the person or people to whom the writer addresses his/her writing.
summarize to capture all the most important parts of the original text (paragraph, story, poem), but express them in a much shorter space, and -- as much as possible -- in the reader's own words
